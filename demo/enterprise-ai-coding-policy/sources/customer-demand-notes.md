# Customer Demand Notes

Use these notes as local research input for the market research agent. They are
deliberately phrased as internal observations, not public claims.

## Enterprise Buyer Patterns

- Security and procurement teams increasingly ask whether AI tools are used in
  software development and whether source code or customer data is sent to
  third-party systems.
- Buyers rarely object to AI coding assistance in principle. They object to
  unmanaged usage, unclear vendor approvals, and a lack of audit evidence.
- The strongest customer-facing answer is not "we never use AI." It is "we use
  approved tools under a documented policy, with human review and restricted
  handling of sensitive data."
- Sales and customer success teams need a short, consistent answer they can use
  during security reviews.

## Useful Policy Signals

- Approved tool list
- Human review before production merge
- Ban on secrets, customer data, and unreleased strategy in prompts
- Clear exception process
- Evidence that the policy is maintained by engineering and security

## Suggested Buyer-Facing Message

We allow AI coding assistance for productivity, but we do not treat generated
code as trusted output. Engineers remain accountable for review, testing, and
compliance with customer data handling rules.
